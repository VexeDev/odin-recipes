Bruschetta

Description

Indulge in the classic Italian antipasto, bruschetta, an appetizer that's as simple as it is elegant. Crisp slices of toasted bread are rubbed with fresh garlic and topped with a vibrant mixture of ripe tomatoes, sweet basil, and a drizzle of rich, fruity extra virgin olive oil. Each bite delivers a satisfying crunch followed by the juicy, fresh flavors of the tomato mixture, accented by a hint of sea salt and a balsamic glaze that adds a sweet and tangy finish. Perfect for a summer gathering or as a start to a gourmet meal, this bruschetta is a timeless dish that celebrates the simplicity and deliciousness of fresh, high-quality ingredients.

Ingredients

  • Baguette or ciabatta bread
  • Ripe tomatoes
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Garlic cloves
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Balsamic glaze or vinegar
  • Sea salt
  • Black pepper

Steps

  1. 1. Preheat your oven to a moderate temperature for toasting the bread.
  2. 2. Slice the baguette or ciabatta into 1/2-inch thick pieces, on a diagonal to increase surface area.
  3. 3. Peel and halve the garlic cloves, and set them aside.
  4. 4. Place the bread slices on a baking sheet and toast in the oven until they are golden and crisp, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. 5. While the bread is toasting, dice the tomatoes and place them in a mixing bowl.
  6. 6. Tear or chop the fresh basil leaves and add them to the tomatoes.
  7. 7. Drizzle the tomato and basil mixture with extra virgin olive oil, add a splash of balsamic glaze or vinegar, and season with sea salt and black pepper to taste. Gently toss to combine.
  8. 8. Once the bread is toasted, remove from the oven and while still warm, rub one side of each slice with the cut side of the garlic cloves for a hint of flavor.
  9. 9. Top each garlic-rubbed bread slice with a generous spoonful of the tomato mixture.
  10. 10. Drizzle with a little more extra virgin olive oil and balsamic glaze if desired, and serve immediately to enjoy the contrast of warm, crispy bread with the cool, refreshing topping.
